What is LΑst WΩrd?

Last Word is a Dead Man's Switch- an application that enables you to share sensitive data (your "will") with recipients (designated by you) after a period of inactivity.

A server is used to transfer a will posthumously or release information to the public.

Last Word performs all encryption and decryption on the client's side. Therefore, all data will be confidential and only available to selected recipients.


Liabilities

Last Word will NOT ask for your personal information such as your real name or email.

The Web server does NOT log identifying user data. The server will not track IP addresses, browser fingerprints, user activities, etc.

The data will only be available to you and your recipients (once the data is released).

What's up with the logo?

The logo for Last Word is a dark red light switch, a representation of the dead man's switch. It is currently in the "off" position, ready to turn on whenever the period of inactivity expires. The face of the light switch is a parchment color with lines on it, representing a last will and testament. When the switch is turned on, the "will" is hidden from view, meaning no one will see it except you and the recipients.
